How We Work

Plain rules, kept without exception.

Built in the field, not in a lab

Nothing gets recommended to you that we haven't already run in a real business — our own. If it didn't survive a Monday morning with a real crew and real customers, you'll never hear about it.

You stay in the driver's seat

Your books stay yours. Your decisions stay yours. We build the machine; you hold the keys. No surveillance, no second-guessing, no dependence.

We do our homework first

Before we ever sit down, we've studied your business the way a buyer would. The first conversation starts at what matters — not at "so, tell me about your company."

Small by design

We take on a handful of owners at a time and treat each one like the reputation of the whole firm rides on the result — because it does.

The Operator Behind It

Kevin Greenspan is not a consultant who read about business in a book. He spent nearly four decades as a practicing chiropractor and business owner — building a healthcare practice in a town of 8,000 that grossed $2 million a year out of 2,000 square feet. Before that: building a car-detailing operation in South Florida, surviving a rental-property loss in 2009 that taught more than any MBA could, and finishing an eight-year degree — undergrad and graduate school stacked on top of each other — in six years, because tuition was money and money was earned.

Today he works with established business owners across Western North Carolina — especially owners thinking about what their business becomes when they're ready to step back — bringing modern systems to Main-Street companies without the Silicon Valley nonsense. You won't find him behind a call center: he meets clients in person, Tuesdays and Thursdays, at Earth Heart in Marion.

The rule that governs everything: if we wouldn't run it in our own family's businesses, we don't put it in yours. The three businesses above aren't clients we found. They're the proof we built first.
